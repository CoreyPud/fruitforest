# Play on Echo shortcuts

Two shortcuts are intended to remain installed:

- **Play on Echo** shares a track, album, or playlist from Apple Music.
- **Echo Quick Play** accepts a typed Alexa-style request.

## Play on Echo

1. Open Apple Music and find a track, album, or playlist.
2. For an album, start one song from that album. This gives the shortcut a
   reliable fallback when iOS does not include a URL in the share payload.
3. Open the item's **More** menu (`...`) and choose **Share**.
4. Choose **Play on Echo** in the share sheet.
5. Choose one of the current FruitForest destinations.
6. Wait for the **Sent to Echo** notification.

The shortcut identifies tracks and albums through Apple's public iTunes lookup
API. Playlist names come from Apple Music's oEmbed API. Before showing the room
picker, it downloads the current target list from the local FruitForest
webhook, then sends the JSON playback request to that same endpoint.

## Echo Quick Play

1. Run **Echo Quick Play** from Shortcuts, Spotlight, Siri, or the menu bar.
2. At **Play what?**, type a natural Alexa request, such as
   `the album Rumours by Fleetwood Mac`.
3. At **Play where?**, choose a speaker or group.
4. Wait for the **Sent to Echo** notification.

## Requirements

- Connect to the home network so the local Home Assistant address is reachable.
- Configure targets under **Settings → Devices & services → FruitForest**.
- Keep **Show in Share Sheet** enabled for **Play on Echo**, with all input
  types selected. Apple Music may share an iTunes product instead of a URL.

## Troubleshooting

- **No URL Specified**: the installed shortcut is an obsolete copy or its
  webhook action lost its URL. Replace it with a newly generated copy.
- **Unknown Variable**: the installed shortcut was generated by the older
  action serializer. Replace it; do not repair the individual action by hand.
- **Notification says `freeform: -> room`**: the room picker worked, but the
  shared item arrived blank and an obsolete shortcut sent it anyway. The
  current shortcut falls back to the current song's album and artist.
- **Notification says `Nothing sent`**: iOS supplied no link and Music had no
  current song. Start a song from the intended album, then share again.
- **No playback, but Sent to Echo appears**: check Home Assistant persistent
  notifications and Alexa Media Player authentication.
- **Shortcut cannot connect**: confirm the device is on the home network and
  Home Assistant is available at its local address.
- **Rooms do not load**: the Shortcut has the wrong webhook URL, Home Assistant
  is unavailable, or the old POST-only YAML package still owns the webhook.
- **New or renamed Echo is missing**: assign its Home Assistant Area and enable
  it under **FruitForest → Configure → Echo devices**. Reopen the Shortcut; no
  rebuild is required.

## Rebuild

Generate placeholder artifacts safe to keep in the repository:

```sh
node tools/generate-shortcuts.mjs
```

For a private build, provide `PLAY_ON_ECHO_WEBHOOK_URL` and optionally
`PLAY_ON_ECHO_OUTPUT_DIR`. Never commit the real webhook URL.
